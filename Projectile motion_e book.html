üìö Theory of Projectile Motion

Projectile Motion is the motion of an object thrown or projected into the air, subject only to the acceleration due to gravity. The object is called a projectile, and its path is called its trajectory.

Key Characteristics:

  • The horizontal component of velocity remains constant (no air resistance)
  • The vertical component of velocity changes due to gravity
  • The trajectory is a parabola
  • Motion can be analyzed as two independent motions: horizontal and vertical
